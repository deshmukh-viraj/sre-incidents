# RB-005: Compliance Audit Event

## What This Incident Looks Like

A regulatory audit has been triggered — PCI-DSS, GDPR, or SOX.
This is NOT a production incident. No services are degraded.
However SRE involvement is required for evidence collection.
Audit tooling generates slightly elevated read traffic on account_ledger.
Compliance events volume will spike in Loki during the audit window.

---

## Context

This system handles three regulatory frameworks:

### PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry)
Governs how cardholder data is stored, processed, transmitted.
QSA (Qualified Security Assessor) audits happen quarterly.
Evidence required: access logs, encryption configs, network diagrams.
SRE role: provide log exports, confirm retention policies are active.

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
Governs EU customer personal data handling.
Audits can be triggered by complaints or scheduled reviews.
Evidence required: data access logs, consent records, retention schedules.
SRE role: confirm PII masking is working, provide data lineage.

### SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley)
Governs financial reporting integrity and IT general controls.
Focuses on: who has access to what, change management, segregation of duties.
Evidence required: deployment logs, access reviews, change approvals.
SRE role: provide deployment history, confirm ITGC controls are active.

---

## What Auditors Need From SRE

### Log Access
Export structured logs from Loki for the requested time window.
Logs must include: timestamp, user_id (pseudonymized), action, service.
Do NOT provide raw PII — confirm masking is applied before export.
Retention: logs must be available for 2555 days (7 years) per config.

### Metrics Evidence
Export Prometheus metrics showing system availability during audit period.
Key metrics: uptime percentage, error rates, access patterns.

### Access Review
List of all users with privileged access to production systems.
Recent changes to access permissions.
Any privilege escalation events from security logs.

### Change Management Records
All deployments in the requested time window.
Approvals for each deployment (who approved, when).
Any emergency changes and their justifications.

---

## How To Respond

### Step 1 — Confirm audit scope
What regulation? What time window? What specific controls are being reviewed?
Do not collect evidence until scope is confirmed in writing.

### Step 2 — Verify log integrity
Confirm logs are present and unmodified for the requested period.
Check compliance_events_total metric for data_access_audit events.
Confirm siem_forwarded field is true on security log records.

### Step 3 — Export evidence without modifying systems
Use read-only queries only during audit.
Do not restart services, change configs, or rotate keys during active audit.
Any changes during audit window must be logged and disclosed.

### Step 4 — Confirm PII handling
Verify pan masking is active in payment logs (first 6, last 4 only).
Verify account_id pseudonymization is consistent.
Verify GDPR legal_basis field is present on all data access records.

### Step 5 — Notify compliance team
Send audit response package to compliance team for review before submission.
Do not submit evidence directly to auditors without compliance sign-off.

---

## What Normal Looks Like During Audit

- compliance_events_total rate slightly elevated (audit tool traffic)
- account_ledger request rate 10-30% above baseline (log queries)
- No errors — audit is read-only
- Audit typically completes within 3 hours

---

## Escalation Signals

Escalate to compliance team immediately if:
- Auditor requests access to production systems directly
- Evidence requested covers a period where logs may be missing
- Audit scope includes a known security incident
- You are asked to make system changes during the audit window
