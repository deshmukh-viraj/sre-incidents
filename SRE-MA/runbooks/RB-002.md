# RB-002: Circuit Breaker Trip

## What This Incident Looks Like

A circuit breaker between two services has opened.
Requests are fast-failing instead of waiting for a degraded downstream.
This protects the system from cascade but means users are seeing errors.
The circuit breaker opening is a symptom — the root cause is always upstream.

---

## Service Context

Circuit breakers exist on these paths:
```
payment_gateway → card_rails        (CB-001)
payment_gateway → account_ledger    (CB-002)
account_ledger  → postgres_primary  (CB-003)
fraud_detector  → redis (ML model)  (CB-004)
```

When a CB opens, the service stops calling the downstream entirely.
Requests fail immediately with 503. This is intentional — it prevents
thread pool exhaustion and gives the downstream time to recover.

---

## Common Root Causes

### 1. Postgres Replica OOM / Failover
Replica runs out of memory, triggers failover to primary.
Connection storm hits primary during failover window.
account_ledger sees timeouts → CB opens → payment_gateway also opens.
Pattern: DB connection pool near exhaustion, replication lag spike.

### 2. Card Rails Outage
External card network goes down or becomes very slow.
payment_gateway CB opens after failure threshold is crossed.
Pattern: 504 errors from card_rails, no internal DB issues.

### 3. Deployment Caused Downstream Failure
New version of a service is crashing or returning 500s.
The calling service detects failures → CB opens.
Pattern: CB opened immediately after a deployment event.

---

## How To Investigate

### Step 1 — Identify which circuit breaker is open
Query the circuit_breaker_state metric.
Value 0 = closed (healthy), 1 = half-open, 2 = open (broken).
Note exactly which service → upstream pair is affected.

### Step 2 — Check the downstream service health
Look at the upstream service that the CB is protecting against.
Is it returning errors? Is it even reachable?
Check its error rate and latency metrics directly.

### Step 3 — Look at DB connection pool
If account_ledger CB is open — check postgres_primary pool utilization.
Pool above 85% = DB pressure is the likely trigger.
Look for HikariPool warnings in Loki logs for account_ledger.

### Step 4 — Check for recent deployments
Was anything deployed to the downstream service recently?
Correlate CB open time with deployment timestamps.

### Step 5 — Check replication lag
If postgres replica is involved, check replication lag metric.
High lag or replica offline = failover in progress.

---

## Remediation Actions

### If downstream is recovering on its own:
Do nothing — let the circuit breaker half-open naturally.
The CB will probe the downstream every 30 seconds automatically.
Monitor the circuit_breaker_state metric for transition to closed.

### If downstream needs help recovering:
Fix the underlying issue first — DB pressure, bad deployment, etc.
Only after downstream is healthy will the CB close.
Forcing the CB closed manually while downstream is still broken
will immediately reopen it and cause another cascade.

### If it is a bad deployment:
Roll back the downstream service first.
Wait for it to return healthy responses.
CB will close automatically within 1-2 probe cycles.

### If DB connection pool caused it:
Hand off to RB-004 workflow for connection exhaustion.
CB will not close until pool pressure is relieved.

---

## What Recovery Looks Like

- circuit_breaker_state returns to 0 (closed)
- Error rate on the affected service drops back below 1%
- Request throughput returns to baseline
- No new CB open events within 5 minutes

---

## Escalation Signals

Escalate if:
- CB has been open more than 15 minutes
- Multiple circuit breakers open simultaneously
- Downstream service is completely unreachable (not just slow)
- DB failover is in progress and primary is also degraded
